Testing Guidelines for Speech Emotion Recognition

A

Prosodic Variability

Speech emotion recognition depends heavily on prosodic variables (pitch contours, speech rate, and energy dynamics) rather than verbal semantics.

B

Acoustic Reference Standards

Speak clearly and with intent. Act out emotions similarly to classical training references (e.g. fast, loud for Anger; soft, slow for Sadness).

C

Microphone Settings

Ensure you are in a quiet room. High background frequency hiss can be mistakenly processed as high energy spectral cues (like Anger or Fear).
